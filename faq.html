Frequently Asked Questions

What is Peterson's Algorithm? ➤
Peterson's Algorithm is a software-based solution to the critical section problem, ensuring mutual exclusion between two processes.
How does mutual exclusion work? ➤
Mutual exclusion ensures that only one thread or process accesses a critical section at a time, preventing race conditions.
Why is the critical section important? ➤
The critical section is important because it contains shared resources that must not be accessed by more than one thread at once to maintain data integrity.
What are the drawbacks of Peterson’s Algorithm? ➤
It only works for two processes, and modern CPUs may reorder instructions, breaking its guarantees unless memory fences are used.
Is Peterson’s Algorithm still used today? ➤
It's mainly used for educational purposes today, as hardware-level solutions like mutexes or semaphores are preferred in real systems.
What does "busy waiting" mean? ➤
Busy waiting occurs when a process constantly checks for a condition without releasing CPU resources, leading to inefficiency.
